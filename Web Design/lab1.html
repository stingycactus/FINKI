DNA extraction is a fundamental process in molecular biology that allows scientists to isolate genetic material from cells. During the procedure, the cell membrane is broken down to release the contents of the cell, including the DNA. Special care must be taken to avoid contamination during the experiment. In many laboratories, the purification step is considered one of the most critical stages of the entire process.

After extraction, the DNA sample can be analyzed using different laboratory techniques. Researchers often measure the DNA concentration to determine the quality of the sample. Sometimes additional purification is required if protein residues remain in the solution. Proper storage is also extremely important to maintain sample stability. For long-term preservation, DNA is commonly stored at very low temperatures in specialized laboratory freezers.
